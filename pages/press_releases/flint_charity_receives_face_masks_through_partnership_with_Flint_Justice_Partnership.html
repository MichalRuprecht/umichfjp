Flint charity receives face masks through partnership with Flint Justice Partnership

Catholic Charities of Shiawassee and Genesee Counties received over 80 face masks through partnership


Catholic Charities of Shiawassee and Genesee Counties received over 80 face masks this Wednesday. FLINT JUSTICE PARTNERSHIP.

Flint, Michigan—Catholic Charities of Shiawassee and Genesee Counties (CCSGC) received over 80 face masks through a partnership between Flint Justice Partnership (FJP) and the Ann Arbor Sewing Center on Wednesday, May 6. CCSGC gave the masks away to Flint residents in an effort to help residents follow CDC guidelines during the COVID-19 pandemic.

“They came just in the nick of time,” CCSGC wrote in a Facebook post. “We had literally given out our last three when they arrived.”

FJP’s community outreach co-chair and U-M LSA freshman Jon Musai coordinated the partnership. FJP’s co-chair and U-M LSA sophomore Michal Ruprecht also helped start the partnership between the two organizations. The initiative is part of FJP’s new resource website to help Flint residents find important resources quickly. Click here to read more about the initiative.

About the Flint Justice Partnership
The Flint Justice Partnership at the University of Michigan-Ann Arbor aims to serve and connect with the Flint community and educate UM students about the Flint water crisis. FJP partners with multiple organizations to advance our mission. FJP was co-founded by Michal Ruprecht, Lindsay Miles, Quinn Nolan, Emily Welch, and Demetrius Ford in October of 2018.
